West Tiling

Hard work. Quality tiles.

Joshua West (owner of West Tiling) has spent years perfecting his trade - tiling. Starting as an apprentice, then stepping out to pursue his own business, Josh is the embodiment of what grit, determination and passion gets you. The brand tries to display these traits, so new clients know what to expect when approaching him for his services.

Approach

From the beginning...

After our initial chats, Josh made it clear that he was interested in having a logo of luxury and class. After some exploration in sketching back and forth, it became clear he was very particular to small details. So, I started leaning into some 'geometric' concepts, which we discovered he resonated with. Once I found the right mark (the graphic part of the logo), finding the right font to complete the logo was the last step before sending it off. The rest of the brand (business card, shirt design, etc.) was all a simple after the groundwork was laid. The mark in the logo was able to be used to make patterns, seen on his business card for example. As for the shirts, it was a matter of balancing simplicity and practicality in the designs (how big is the mark, do we use a pattern, do we use the text only or the mark only).

What was tough in this project?

When we started, Josh wasn't too sure what he wanted his logo to look like. All he knew is that when people saw 'West Tiling', they knew it was high-end. This is always challenging for a designer, because that leaves an ocean of different styles / ideas to pick from. Not picking the right one can be frustrating for both parties and ultimately, waste both of our time.

How did I tackle this?

"Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication." ‐ da Vinci. Trying to create a brand that resembles high class and years of perfecting in a simple form is quite hard! However, it becomes easier once you can establish an understanding between what is expected of a brand and what you like/don't like about others. Simply going through different ideas, looking at different types of directions we could go (classic, current/trendy, old-style) gave clarity and ideas were being born. I learned here that sitting with Josh and breaking down different brands was a strong way to generate ideas of our own.

Blayne Fletcher Bowling
