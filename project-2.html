Blayne Fletcher Bowling
Professional Athlete
- May 10th 2021
BF Bowling

Australian representative - as seen in Rollin' Thunder and Tenpin Premier League

If you follow bowling at a professional level at all, you would have certainly heard of Blayne Fletcher. Junior International Champion in 2016, his career in bowling has been rising since seeing great success as a teenager. He can now be seen on TV in the TPL (Tenpin Premier League) or representing Australia internationally!

Creating something iconic.

Blayne wanted something simple, sharp, and professional for viewers and sponsors to see. Looking at the likes of the Nike Jordan for inspiration, or much closer to home, Jason Belmonte's brand. It needed to be versatile ‐ so locking it into one colour scheme wasn't viable. It couldn't be complex ‐ a simple form that just clicks in peoples head (from any distance of viewing it) was what we were after. So with this in mind, we explored some obvious and not so obvious ties to bowling in his brand. After some time, Blayne decided that he liked the more subtle and less juvenile designs that were coming up. In the end, that's what we ended up striving for across all his material ‐ simple, sharp, professional, subtle and mature.

What was tough in this project?

There is a lot of pressure for someone in Blayne's shoes. He's seen not only by Australia, but across the world when he represents internationally. When you want to be taken seriously at this level, it's very important to feel comfortable with your brand. So, finding the right logo, colours, fonts, etc. to represent him was quite a hard thing to pictureâ€¦ or even consider attempting.

How did I tackle this?

The key learning here was spending more time than usual unboxing what Blayne wanted from his brand. It's hard to think of a face that can be perceived universally the same to a large audience. So understanding how Blayne wanted to be perceived, as closely as I could (without being in his head), was the most important part of this process. After repeating what I understand to him in meetings and him acknowledging, I am comfortable to then explore concepts. That way, I can create and explain how I incorporate his principles in each design to make him comfortable and me happy!
