Brand Design
Jake Dangerfield Fitness
Personal Trainer
- October 9th, 2022
JD Fitness

Healthy body, healthy mind.

Personal training is a hard gig. It's super competitive, finding clients is quite hard and maintaining them is even harder. When Jake worked as my personal trainer, however, I thought his methods / energy were top shelf and not recognised enough. Henceforth, I decided that I would work with him to make a strong brand to display the elite service that he provides.

Joining the guild ‐ Jake's clients.

At first, Jake didn't really know what he wanted his brand to look like, as he had just started going solo. What he did have, however, was lots of knowledge in personal training. This was evident through his knowledge of the human body, experience in training a wide variety of clients and the journey he's had in weight loss himself. He understands the mental warfare that comes with fitness and loves to encourage his clients to fight ‐ push through the mental chains that limit them from reaching their true potential. Displaying this grit / warfare, but also the results and mentorship that come with training, was the crux of this brand. I worked with Jake through concepts that he liked, constraints from previous employment, and together we discovered what worked for his brand.

What was tough in this project?

Jake's brand had a couple of red zones to consider straight away. He'd just left the company that represented him in personal training, so steering clear of anything that might associate to that brand (colours, mark, typography, etc) was required. Also, he performs his training in Jetts, so ensuring no overlap in branding there was also important. Having a distinct brand that can work alongside Jetts, though, was a nice bonus here.

How did I tackle this?

It was an early learning for this project - knowing about the brand limitations I had before I even started designing is imperative. Had Jake not told me about any red zones with other companies (other than the obvious Jetts which I could deduce), I may have incidentally included colours or fonts from the other brand. That would have wasted lots of my discovery time and crucial time for Jake, as he really needed a brand. Early days are important for him, because he needs to maintain his current clientele before he looks to find more people he can help!
